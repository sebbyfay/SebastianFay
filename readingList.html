Reading List

I love to read, although, like most, I don't think I read enough. I started reading books again about 3 years ago to kill time at my slow summer job. I read almost exclusively non-fiction books and I try to reach a book each month (with about a 60% success rate).

I think its important to approach each book with an open mind, and be willing to have your opinions and perspectives changed. I find that reading non-fiction often entails navigating the differing ideas from well qualified and celebrated authors, which I love. With every book I read I concede that everything I know on a given topic might be wrong, and I open myself up to the ideas and findings of the author, while maintaining the stance that they might also be wrong.

Here are the books that I have read, and some that I intend to read soon. There is no order to this list.

  • Creativity Inc. - Ed Catmull, Amy Wallace

  • Behind the Cloud - Marc Benioff, Carlye Adler

  • My Morning Routine - Benjamin Spall, Michael Xander

  • The Ride of a Lifetime - Bob Iger

  • When Genius Failed - Roger Lowenstein

  • The Black Swan - Nassim Micholas Taleb

  • The Lean Startup - Eric Ries

  • Rich Dad Poor Dad - Robert T. Kiyosaki

  • Blitzscaling - Reid Hoffman, Chris Yeh

  • Crossing the Chasm - Geoffrey A. Moore

  • The Innovator's Dilemma - Clayton M. Christensen

  • The Airbnb Story - Leigh Gallagher

  • The Happines Hypothesis - Jonathan Haidt

  • Sapiens - Yuval Noah Harari

  • Grit - Angela Duckworth

  • Zero to One - Peter Thiel, Blake Masters

  • The Quants - Scott Patterson

  • The New Market Wizards - Jack D. Schwager
